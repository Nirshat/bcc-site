Bachelor Of Elementary Education

The Bachelor of Elementary Education (B.Ed.) is an undergraduate degree program designed to prepare individuals for a career in teaching at the elementary level. It focuses on developing the knowledge, skills, and competencies necessary to educate and inspire young learners in the primary grades, typically ranging from kindergarten to grade six, depending on the educational system of the country.

The program provides aspiring teachers with a comprehensive understanding of child development, educational theories, pedagogical techniques, and subject-specific content knowledge necessary for teaching multiple subjects in the elementary curriculum. The curriculum typically includes courses in language arts, mathematics, science, social studies, arts and crafts, physical education, and health education.

During the course of their studies, students in the Bachelor of Elementary Education program learn about effective teaching strategies, classroom management techniques, assessment and evaluation methods, educational psychology, inclusive education, and the integration of technology into teaching practices. They also gain practical experience through supervised teaching practicums and fieldwork in elementary schools, where they have the opportunity to apply their learning in real classroom settings.



Bachelor of Science in Information Technology

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (B.S. IT) is an undergraduate degree program that focuses on the study of technology and its application in the field of information systems and computer science. It is designed to equip students with a strong foundation in both theoretical concepts and practical skills necessary for a career in the IT industry.

The program typically covers a wide range of subjects related to information technology, including computer programming, database management, networking, web development, software engineering, systems analysis, cybersecurity, and data management. Students learn how to design, develop, implement, and maintain various software applications, computer systems, and IT infrastructure.

Throughout the program, students gain a solid understanding of programming languages, algorithms, data structures, computer architecture, operating systems, and software development methodologies. They also acquire knowledge in areas such as database design and management, network administration, web technologies, mobile app development, and IT project management.
